# Musahm Competitive Battle Cards

> **Audience**: Sales team, SEs, account executives
> **Classification**: Internal use only -- do not share with prospects
> **Last updated**: 2026-04-02
> **Owner**: Pre-Sales Engineering
> **Framework**: Extended Battlecard 7-Section Structure (Overview / Positioning / Landmines / Differentiation / Trap-Setting / Proof / Offer)
> **Aligned to**: Canonical Positioning Statement (`positioning-statement.md`)

---

## How to Use These Cards

Each card follows an extended version of the 7-Section Battlecard Structure, adapted for Musahm:

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| 1. Overview | What they are, who they target, when you will see them |
| 2. Positioning | Their pitch vs. our counter -- the narrative battle |
| 3. Landmines | Questions that expose their weakness -- ask these in discovery |
| 4. Differentiation | What we have that they do not (with proof). What they have that we do not (with honest handling) |
| 5. Trap-Setting | Questions to plant in the buyer's mind that shift evaluation criteria in our favor |
| 6. Proof Points | Client references, demo moments, and data that close the credibility gap |
| 7. Offer Packaging | How to position our pricing and packaging against theirs |

**FIA Rule**: Every claim follows the Fact-Impact-Act framework. Every claim is factual and verifiable. Never trash a competitor -- acknowledge their strengths, then redirect to where Musahm wins. Credibility is our most valuable asset in the room.

**Rule of thumb**: If you cannot connect a feature to a business outcome the prospect cares about, do not mention the feature.

# Battle Card 1: Majles.tech

## 1. Quick Snapshot

Majles.tech is a modern, startup-energy governance platform targeting mid-to-large Saudi companies. They lead with English-forward bilingual UX and position around "injecting energy and engagement" into board operations. Their product covers meeting management, resolutions, and board collaboration but lacks the shareholder management and document management depth that Musahm provides.

**ملخص سريع**: منصة حوكمة حديثة بطابع شركات ناشئة، تستهدف الشركات المتوسطة والكبيرة. تركز على إدارة الاجتماعات والقرارات لكنها تفتقر إلى إدارة المساهمين وإدارة المستندات المتقدمة.

## 2. When You'll See Them

- Deals involving **listed companies** or large private companies with active boards
- Prospects who have a young, tech-forward General Secretary or Board Secretary
- RFPs that emphasize "board engagement" or "digital transformation of governance"
- Companies already evaluating Western governance tools and looking for a local alternative

## 3. Their Pitch

"We modernize board operations with real-time collaboration, task tracking, and performance analytics -- all in a platform built for the region."

**عرضهم**: "نحن نُحدّث عمليات مجلس الإدارة بتعاون فوري، وتتبع المهام، وتحليلات الأداء -- في منصة مصممة للمنطقة."

## 4. Our Counter

> **English**: "Majles is a solid meeting management tool. But governance is bigger than meetings. When your board approves a capital increase, who tracks the share transfer? When a shareholder needs formal communication, who sends it? When you need a complete audit trail from board decision to signed contract to filed document -- that is what Musahm was built for. We don't just manage meetings. We manage the company."
>
> **Arabic**: "مجلس أداة جيدة لإدارة الاجتماعات. لكن الحوكمة أكبر من الاجتماعات. عندما يوافق مجلس إدارتكم على زيادة رأس المال، من يتتبع نقل الأسهم؟ عندما يحتاج مساهم إلى تواصل رسمي، من يرسله؟ عندما تحتاجون إلى سجل تدقيق كامل من قرار المجلس إلى العقد الموقّع إلى المستند المحفوظ -- هذا ما بُنيت مساهم من أجله. نحن لا ندير اجتماعات فقط. نحن ندير الشركة."

## 7. Win Strategy

1. **Lead with Shareholder Registry**: In the first 5 minutes of any demo against Majles, show the shareholder registry. Ask: "How do you manage your shareholder records today?" The answer is always Excel or paper. Show Musahm's registry, then ask: "Can Majles do this?" The answer is no. This reframes the entire evaluation from "meeting management" to "full governance."

2. **Expand the definition of governance**: Majles wins when the evaluation is scoped to meetings and resolutions. Musahm wins when the evaluation includes shareholder management, document lifecycle, and compliance. During discovery, map the prospect's full governance workflow -- from board meeting to shareholder communication to filed document. Once they see the full picture, Majles looks like a subset.

3. **Play the institutional Arabic card**: If the prospect is a traditional Saudi company (family-owned, conservative board), Majles's startup energy and English-forward branding can feel foreign. Position Musahm as: "Built by Saudis, for Saudi governance culture. Arabic-first, not Arabic-added."

| "How important is it that governance documents are organized by governance function rather than stored in generic folders?" | "ما أهمية أن تكون وثائق الحوكمة منظّمة حسب وظيفة الحوكمة بدلاً من تخزينها في مجلدات عامة؟" | Plants the DMS evaluation criteria -- Majles has only a basic doc hub |
| "Do you need to send formal shareholder notifications directly from the governance platform, or is email sufficient?" | "هل تحتاجون لإرسال إشعارات رسمية للمساهمين من منصة الحوكمة مباشرة، أم أن البريد الإلكتروني كافٍ؟" | SMS communications is a Musahm-only capability |

## 9. Killer Question

> **Ask the prospect**: "When your board approves a capital increase or share transfer, how does that decision flow to your shareholder registry today? Is it the same system, or does someone manually update a separate record?"
>
> **اسأل العميل**: "عندما يوافق مجلس إدارتكم على زيادة رأس مال أو نقل أسهم، كيف ينتقل هذا القرار إلى سجل المساهمين اليوم؟ هل هو نفس النظام، أم أن شخصًا يحدّث سجلًا منفصلًا يدويًا؟"

**Why this works**: It exposes the gap between "meeting tool" and "governance platform." No matter how they answer, the follow-up is: "Musahm connects the board decision directly to the shareholder registry. One system, one source of truth."

## 9. Proof Points

- **Client reference**: Al-Ufuq Educational, Miral Medical, Amwaj -- operating on Musahm with full governance. Vault in active beta.
- **Demo moment**: Live shareholder registry walkthrough -- show share transfer flow from board resolution to updated registry entry
- **Feature demo**: SMS shareholder notification sent live during the demo -- prospect sees the message arrive in real time

## 10. When to Walk Away

- The prospect's **only** need is board meeting scheduling and task tracking, with no interest in shareholder management or document lifecycle. Majles is a better fit for pure meeting management.
- The prospect has a large, tech-forward board that values real-time collaboration features (co-editing, live commenting) above all else.
- The prospect explicitly wants board member performance analytics as their primary buying criterion.

In these cases, compete but don't overextend. Acknowledge the fit and focus your pipeline on prospects who need full governance.

# Battle Card 2: Ebana DMS

## 1. Quick Snapshot

Ebana is a Saudi IT consultancy that offers custom-built document management solutions as part of broader enterprise IT services. They are English-dominant, enterprise-priced (SAR 100,000+ implementations), and deploy through bespoke consulting engagements. Their DMS is generic file storage with metadata tagging and role-based access -- it has no governance context.

**ملخص سريع**: شركة استشارات تقنية سعودية تقدم حلول إدارة مستندات مخصصة كجزء من خدمات تقنية أوسع. باهظة الثمن (أكثر من 100,000 ريال للتنفيذ)، وبطيئة في النشر، ولا تملك أي سياق حوكمة.

- Deals where the IT team -- not the governance team -- owns the buying decision
- Prospects who already use Ebana for other IT services (email migration, infrastructure, etc.)

1. **Make them do the math**: Ask the prospect to estimate the total cost of an Ebana implementation: licensing, consulting fees, customization hours, ongoing support, and the internal team time to manage the project. Musahm wins when the buying decision sits with the Board Secretary, General Counsel, or CEO. During discovery, identify the governance stakeholders and ensure they are in the evaluation. A governance buyer does not want to manage a custom IT project -- they want a working system.

3. **Demo the governance context live**: Show a board resolution in Musahm, then open Vault and navigate to the governance workspace where related documents are organized. Show the workflow history and audit trail on a document within that workspace. Then ask: "Can your current DMS organize documents by governance function with built-in workflow tracking, or would you need to build it from scratch?"

## 9. Killer Question

> **Ask the prospect**: "When you store a board-approved contract in your DMS today, can you trace it back to the specific board resolution that authorized it -- automatically, without someone manually filing it?"
>
> **اسأل العميل**: "عندما تحفظون عقدًا معتمدًا من المجلس في نظام إدارة المستندات اليوم، هل يمكنكم تتبعه للعودة إلى قرار المجلس المحدد الذي أذن به -- تلقائيًا، بدون أن يقوم شخص بالحفظ اليدوي؟"

**Why this works**: No generic DMS can do this. It is a governance-specific requirement that only a DMS built for governance like Vault can deliver. The answer is always "no" or "we would need to build that," which opens the door to: "Vault does this out of the box."

## 9. Proof Points

- **Cost comparison**: Vault subscription vs. Ebana's SAR 100,000+ implementation -- prepare a side-by-side TCO slide
- **Time to value**: "Vault deploys in minutes. Here is a live instance we set up for this demo this morning."
- **Governance link demo**: Resolution --> document workflow --> audit trail, shown end-to-end in under 3 minutes
- **Client reference**: Existing Musahm clients who previously evaluated custom DMS solutions and chose Vault

## 10. When to Walk Away

- The prospect has a **hard regulatory requirement for on-premise deployment** that cannot be met by cloud with data residency
- The prospect's primary need is **enterprise content management** (ECM) at massive scale (millions of documents, complex taxonomy, records management) with no governance requirement
- The prospect has an existing Ebana relationship covering multiple IT services, and the DMS is a small add-on to a larger contract -- the switching cost is political, not technical

# Battle Card 3: Diligent

## 1. Quick Snapshot

Diligent is the largest global governance technology provider, serving primarily Fortune 500 companies, large financial institutions, and multinational boards. They offer comprehensive board management, entity management, and compliance solutions. They are not currently active in the Saudi SMB market but represent a credible enterprise threat for large listed companies. Pricing is enterprise-tier (typically $50,000-$200,000+ annually).

**ملخص سريع**: أكبر مزود عالمي لتقنية الحوكمة، يخدم بشكل أساسي شركات فورتشن 500 والمؤسسات المالية الكبرى. غير نشط حاليًا في سوق الشركات الصغيرة والمتوسطة السعودية، لكنه يمثل تهديدًا محتملًا للشركات الكبيرة المدرجة.

## 2. When You'll See Them

- Prospects with **multinational operations** or international board members
- Deals where a **Big Four consulting firm** (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) is advising on governance transformation
