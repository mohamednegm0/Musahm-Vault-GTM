## 2. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Governance Education, "حوكمة الوثائق"

**Purpose:** Educate Saudi business leaders on document governance standards, common gaps, and what proper governance actually looks like. Position Musahm as the authority on Saudi corporate governance documentation.

**Description:** These posts teach without selling. They expose the real documentation challenges Saudi companies face with enough specificity that the reader recognizes their own situation. The product may appear at the end as a natural resolution, but the value is in the education itself.

**Frequency:** 4 posts per month (1 per week on average)

**Example topics:**

- What the new Companies Law actually requires for meeting documentation
- The difference between file storage and document governance
- How to build a document retention policy for a Saudi LLC
- What auditors look for in governance documentation (and what they reject)

---

### Pillar 2: Saudi Corporate Law & Vision 2030, "المعيار الجديد"

**Purpose:** Connect governance to the regulatory environment driving demand. Demonstrate that Musahm tracks and responds to Saudi regulatory changes.

**Description:** These posts cover new regulations, CMA requirements, PDPL implications for document handling, and Vision 2030 governance mandates. They position Musahm as the natural response to the compliance landscape. Vision 2030 is referenced through specific mandates, never as a hashtag without substance.

**Frequency:** 3 posts per month

**Example topics:**

- PDPL implications for how companies share and store governance documents
- New Companies Law requirements for unlisted companies and what they mean for documentation
- How Vision 2030's corporate governance objectives translate to daily document management
- CMA disclosure requirements and what they demand from your document systems

---

### Pillar 3: Behind-the-Scenes / Builder Stories, "بنينا"

**Purpose:** Humanize the brand. Show the people, decisions, and values behind Musahm. Build trust through transparency.

**Description:** These posts share the engineering decisions, design philosophy, and team culture behind building a Saudi governance platform. They reinforce the "built from scratch" narrative with authentic stories. No startup energy or motivational filler. This is the voice of builders describing what they built and why.

**Frequency:** 2 posts per month

**Example topics:**

- Why we chose to build an Arabic-first UX instead of translating an English one
- The engineering decision behind our five-role permission model
- How our team researches Saudi corporate law before writing a single line of code
- A day in the life of a Musahm product engineer building for Saudi governance

**Purpose:** Build social proof through real outcomes. Show that Saudi companies are using and benefiting from Musahm and Vault.

**Description:** These posts share anonymized deployment stories, aggregate usage metrics, and (with permission) named client outcomes. No fabricated testimonials. If client permission is not yet granted, the post uses anonymized sector-specific scenarios based on real deployment patterns. Every post requiring client attribution is flagged with a "NEEDS CLIENT INPUT" marker.

#### Post 2.1, Sunday, 9:30 AM

**Pillar:** Governance Education
**Topic:** Why shared drives are not governance
**Format:** Text-only (long-form)

**Arabic:**

المجلد الشبكي يحفظ الملفات. هذا كل ما يفعله.

لا يعرف الفرق بين محضر اجتماع وعرض تقديمي. لا يسجّل من فتح الملف أو من عدّله. لا يربط الوثيقة بسياقها. ولا يمنعك من حذف ملف بالخطأ بدون أي أثر.

لكن كثيرًا من الشركات السعودية تعتمد على المجلدات الشبكية كنظام إدارة وثائق. والسبب مفهوم: كانت الخيارات إما أنظمة أجنبية مكلفة ومصممة لأسواق أخرى، أو حلول محلية لا تتجاوز التخزين الأساسي.

الفرق بين التخزين والحوكمة واضح:

• التخزين يحفظ الملف. الحوكمة تعرف من وصل إليه ومتى ولماذا.
• التخزين يضع الملف في مجلد. الحوكمة تربطه بالقرار والاجتماع والمساهم المعني، وتحمي الوثيقة بصلاحيات وسجل تدقيق.

هذا هو الفرق الذي بنينا Vault لتحقيقه.

**English:**

A shared drive stores files. That is all it does.

It does not know the difference between a board minute and a presentation. It does not log who opened or edited a file. It does not link a document to its context. And it does not prevent accidental deletion without a trace.

Yet many Saudi companies rely on shared drives as their document management system. The reason is understandable: the alternatives were either expensive foreign systems designed for other markets, or local solutions that did not go beyond basic storage.

The difference between storage and governance is clear:

- Storage keeps a file. Governance knows who accessed it, when, and why.
- Storage puts a file in a folder. Governance links it to the decision, the meeting, and the relevant shareholder.
- Storage allows anyone to delete. Governance protects the document with permissions and an audit trail.

This is the difference Vault was built to deliver.

The Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) did not just regulate personal data. It changed how sensitive documents must be managed within companies.

Three requirements directly affect document management:

First: access control. A company must know who accesses which document and why. Shared drives open to everyone are no longer acceptable.

Second: proof of chain of custody. When a leak or dispute occurs, the company must have a trail showing who accessed the document, when, and how.

Third: limiting uncontrolled sharing. Sending sensitive documents as unprotected email attachments violates the spirit of the law.

These requirements are not theoretical. They are obligations that demand tools designed to fulfill them.
