> **Voice:** Confident Saudi builder. Direct, personal, never salesy. Arabic primary, English secondary. Every message ends with a clear next step.

## 6. Broadcast Best Practices

### WhatsApp Business API vs. Personal

| Factor                | WhatsApp Business App (Personal)         | WhatsApp Business API                                                |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Best for**          | 1-to-1 sales conversations, <50 contacts | Broadcast campaigns, >50 contacts, automation                        |
| **Template messages** | Not required                             | Required for initiating conversations. Must be pre-approved by Meta. |
| **Broadcast limit**   | 256 contacts per list                    | Unlimited (within quality tier)                                      |
| **Automation**        | Basic auto-replies                       | Full CRM integration, chatbots, sequenced messages                   |
| **Recommendation**    | Use for personal follow-ups after demo   | Use for all beta invitation and follow-up campaigns                  |

For Vault beta launch, use the API for broadcast campaigns and the Business App for personal follow-ups from the sales team.

### Template Message Approval: Saudi Arabic Tips

1. **Submit in formal Arabic (فصحى).** Meta reviewers flag colloquial Arabic more often. Use "السلام عليكم" not "هلا" or "مرحبا يا."
2. **Avoid promotional language in the template itself.** Frame the message as informational/utility ("We launched a new product for governance document management") rather than promotional ("Get exclusive access now!"). Promotional templates face stricter review.
3. **Include the business name** (مساهم / Musahm) in the template body, not just the header.
4. **Keep variables to a minimum.** Meta recommends no more than 3 variables per template. Use: `{{1}}` for name, `{{2}}` for company, `{{3}}` for link.
5. **Submit both Arabic and English as separate templates**, not one bilingual template. Each language gets its own approval.
6. **Allow 2-3 business days** for template review. Submit templates at least one week before launch.
7. **Category selection:** Use "Marketing" for beta invitations and "Utility" for post-demo follow-ups. Utility templates have higher delivery rates.

### Timing Recommendations

| Day           | Window (AST)        | Rationale                                                                          |
| ------------- | ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sunday**    | 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM | KSA business week start. Decision-makers reviewing priorities. Highest open rates. |
| **Tuesday**   | 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM   | Mid-week follow-up window. Post-lunch, before end-of-day.                          |
| **Wednesday** | 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM | Good for second follow-ups. Avoid Thursday (pre-weekend wind-down).                |

**Avoid:** Thursday after 2 PM, Friday (weekend), Saturday (weekend), any day after 6 PM, Ramadan daytime hours (send after Iftar if during Ramadan).

### Do's and Don'ts for KSA B2B WhatsApp

**Do:**

- Open with "السلام عليكم"; it is expected in Saudi business communication, even on WhatsApp
- Identify yourself and your company in the first line
- Keep messages under 500 characters when possible (2-3 short paragraphs maximum)
- End every message with exactly one clear next step (link, question, or call-to-action)
- Respond within 2 hours during business hours if a prospect replies
- Use voice notes for follow-ups after a relationship is established (common in Saudi B2B)
- Space follow-up messages at least 48 hours apart
- Respect "read but no reply" as a soft signal; do not send more than 4 messages without a response

**Don't:**

- Send messages before 9:00 AM or after 8:00 PM AST
- Use emoji clusters or exclamation marks (one emoji maximum, and only if it adds clarity)
- Send PDFs or large attachments on first contact; use links instead
- Add prospects to WhatsApp groups without permission
- Use "Dear Sir/Madam" language. WhatsApp is conversational, not email
- Forward the same message to multiple people in a way that looks like mass-blast (personalize the name and company)
- Send audio messages on first contact
- Follow up on the same day if the message was read but not replied to
