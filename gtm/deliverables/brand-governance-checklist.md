# Brand Governance Checklist: Pre-Publish Gate

> **Framework:** 14-Item Pre-Publish Gate
> **Scope:** Every piece of customer-facing content before it goes live. Marketing, sales, partner, social, ads, email.
> **Pass threshold:** 14/14. No exceptions. Fix before publishing.
> **Owner:** Marketing lead reviews. CEO signs off on P1 content (landing page, ads, press).
> **Date:** 2026-04-02

---

## The 14-Item Gate

### Section A: Accuracy (Items 1-4)

These protect against false claims. One slip here can invalidate the entire GTM effort.

| #   | Check                           | What to Verify                                                                                | FAIL Examples                                                                                                                                 |
| --- | ------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **No false product claims**     | Every feature mentioned exists in the current build. Cross-check against product truth table. | Claiming "semantic search" (it is a stub). Claiming "mobile app" (it is a responsive web app). Claiming IP address logging (not implemented). |
| 2   | **No unsourced statistics**     | Every number has a verifiable source. If you cannot cite it, remove it.                       | "95% of Saudi companies" without MOC data. Any percentage without a source link or footnote.                                                  |
| 3   | **Correct product terminology** | Use exact feature names. No synonyms that imply different capabilities.                       | "AI-powered search" when the feature is keyword search. "Bank-grade encryption" without specifying the actual standard.                       |
| 4   | **Beta status honest**          | Content clearly communicates beta state where relevant. No implying GA availability.          | Saying "trusted by hundreds of companies" during beta. Omitting "beta" from signup flows.                                                     |

### Section B: Voice & Brand (Items 5-8)

These protect brand consistency and prevent AI-generated tone from leaking through.

| #   | Check                        | What to Verify                                                                                                                        | FAIL Examples                                                                                                                    |
| --- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5   | **Voice guide compliance**   | Content matches Brand Voice Matrix (4 attributes). No prohibited terms.                                                               | Using "revolutionary," "game-changing," "leverage," "supercharge," "optimal," "smart and secure," "best-in-class."               |
| 6   | **Zero AI writing patterns** | No "Not X, but Y" constructions. No Rule of Three anaphora. No triple-adjective pileups. No em dash overuse (max 2 per piece).        | "Not just storage, but governance." "One that knows... one that prevents... and one that proves." "Smart, secure, and seamless." |
| 7   | **Arabic quality**           | MSA (Modern Standard Arabic). No colloquial. No literal translation from English. Natural sentence structure. Western numerals (0-9). | Egyptian or Gulf dialect. Sentences that read like Google Translate. Arabic numerals (٠-٩) in body text.                         |
| 8   | **Tagline present**          | Every major content piece ends with "حوكمة سعودية. منصة عالمية." or its English equivalent.                                           | Missing tagline. Using a modified or shortened version.                                                                          |

### Section C: Regulatory & Legal (Items 9-11)

These protect against legal exposure in the Saudi market.

| #   | Check                                  | What to Verify                                                                                                                                                                              | FAIL Examples                                                                            |
| --- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 9   | **No "first" or "only" claims**        | Unless legally defensible with documented evidence, never claim market exclusivity. "Shareholder registry built in" is a feature fact. "The only platform with..." is an exclusivity claim. | "أول منصة سعودية" (first Saudi platform). "The only governance solution."                |
| 10  | **Regulatory references accurate**     | Companies Law article numbers correct. CMA references current. No implying Musahm is CMA-certified or SAMA-approved unless true.                                                            | Citing wrong article number. Saying "CMA-compliant" when no formal certification exists. |
| 11  | **No banned hashtags or associations** | Do not use #رؤية2030 or #Vision2030 (government-owned). Do not imply government endorsement.                                                                                                | #رؤية2030 in any social post. "Endorsed by" or "approved by" any government body.        |

### Section D: Technical & Format (Items 12-14)

These protect production quality.

| #   | Check                     | What to Verify                                                                                                                              | FAIL Examples                                                                                                   |
| --- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 12  | **Links work**            | Every URL in the content resolves to a live page. No placeholders in published content.                                                     | Sitelinks pointing to /vault/features when the page does not exist. [URL] placeholders left in published email. |
| 13  | **Bilingual consistency** | AR and EN versions say the same thing. No feature mentioned in one language but missing in the other. Feature names match across languages. | AR version mentions 6 workspaces, EN version says 4. AR uses "مدير" for Admin role, EN uses "Manager."          |
| 14  | **Contact info current**  | Phone numbers, emails, WhatsApp links, Cal.com booking links all tested and working.                                                        | Placeholder [PHONE] in published content. Cal.com link expired. Email bouncing.                                 |

## Pre-Publish Review Process

| Step | Action                                                            | Owner                                                      | Time              |
| ---- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| 1    | Author self-checks against all 14 items                           | Content creator                                            | Before submission |
| 2    | Peer review: second pair of eyes on accuracy (items 1-4)          | Another team member                                        | 30 minutes        |
| 3    | Marketing lead reviews voice + brand (items 5-8)                  | Marketing lead                                             | 30 minutes        |
| 4    | Legal/regulatory check (items 9-11) if content is new or modified | Marketing lead or external counsel                         | 1-2 hours         |
| 5    | Technical check: links, format, bilingual parity (items 12-14)    | Content creator                                            | 15 minutes        |
| 6    | Final sign-off                                                    | Marketing lead (standard) or CEO (high-visibility content) | 15 minutes        |

---

## High-Visibility Content (CEO Sign-Off Required)

These content types require CEO approval before publishing:

- Landing page (any version)
- Google Ads copy (any campaign)
- Press releases or media quotes
- Partner kit materials (external distribution)
- Pricing page or tier structure
- Crisis communications (P1/P2)
- Any content mentioning specific client names

## Common Failures (From M0-M4 Audit History)

These patterns were found and fixed during the GTM build. Watch for recurrence:

| Pattern                    | Where It Was Found                   | Fix Applied                                      |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------ |
| "Semantic search" claimed  | Blog posts, landing page, sales deck | Changed to "keyword search" (semantic is a stub) |
| IP address logging claimed | Video script                         | Removed entirely (not implemented)               |
| OTP described as SMS       | Multiple files                       | Changed to "email OTP" (current implementation)  |
| "أول منصة سعودية" (first)  | Landing page, social posts           | Removed -- unprovable                            |
| 95% statistic unsourced    | Google ads, social                   | Removed or replaced with descriptive language    |
| "30 beta seats" vs "50"    | Conflicting across files             | Standardized to 30 everywhere                    |
| #رؤية2030 hashtag          | LinkedIn strategy, social posts      | Removed -- government-owned                      |
