# Musahm MEDDIC Sales Qualification Checklist

> **Framework:** MEDDPICC adapted for KSA governance SaaS
> **Audience:** Sales team, account executives, pre-sales engineers
> **Use:** Score every opportunity before committing resources. Minimum 60/100 to advance past discovery.
> **Last updated:** 2026-04-02

---

## Scoring Summary

| Element | Weight | Score Range | Minimum to Advance |
|---------|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| **M** -- Metrics | 15 | 0-15 | 8 |
| **E** -- Economic Buyer | 15 | 0-15 | 10 |
| **D** -- Decision Criteria | 15 | 0-15 | 8 |
| **D** -- Decision Process | 10 | 0-10 | 5 |
| **I** -- Identify Pain | 20 | 0-20 | 12 |
| **C** -- Champion | 15 | 0-15 | 8 |
| **C** -- Competition | 10 | 0-10 | 5 |
| **TOTAL** | 100 | 0-100 | **60** |

**Score Interpretation:**
- 80-100: Strong deal. Commit full resources, fast-track demo.
- 60-79: Qualified. Proceed with standard sales cycle.
- 40-59: Weak. Nurture only -- do not commit SE time until score improves.
- Below 40: Disqualify. Move to marketing drip. Revisit in 6 months.

## M -- Metrics (15 points)

**The question:** Can the prospect quantify what governance problems cost them today?

### Discovery Questions

| Question (EN) | Question (AR) | What You Learn |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| "How much time does your Board Secretary spend preparing for each board meeting?" | "كم من الوقت يقضيه أمين المجلس في التحضير لكل اجتماع مجلس إدارة؟" | Time cost of manual governance |
| "How many hours did it take to compile records for your last audit or regulatory review?" | "كم ساعة استغرق تجميع السجلات لآخر تدقيق أو مراجعة تنظيمية؟" | Audit preparation pain |
| "Have you ever had a shareholder dispute related to communication or record-keeping?" | "هل واجهتم نزاعاً مع مساهم يتعلق بالتواصل أو حفظ السجلات؟" | Legal exposure cost |
| "How many people touch the shareholder registry when a share transfer happens?" | "كم شخصاً يتدخل في سجل المساهمين عند نقل أسهم؟" | Process inefficiency |

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 15 | Prospect can quantify: hours lost, audit cost, legal exposure amount, or regulatory fine risk in SAR |
| 10 | Prospect acknowledges pain and can describe it qualitatively ("it takes days," "we had a close call") |
| 5 | Prospect agrees governance is important but cannot tie it to measurable impact |
| 0 | "We are fine. No problems." |

## E -- Economic Buyer (15 points)

**The question:** Who writes the check, and do we have access?

### Key Personas

| Persona | Typical Title (KSA) | Budget Authority | How to Identify |
|---------|---------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Primary EB** | CEO / Managing Director (المدير التنفيذي) | Final sign-off on SaaS subscriptions | Ask: "Who approves new software purchases at your company?" |
| **Secondary EB** | CFO / Finance Director (المدير المالي) | Budget allocation, ROI evaluation | Often the person asking "how much does this cost?" |
| **Influencer** | Board Secretary (أمين المجلس) | Recommends, does not approve | The person who found Musahm and is championing it internally |

### Discovery Questions

| Question (EN) | Question (AR) |
|---------------|---------------|
| "Who has final authority to approve a governance platform investment?" | "من يملك الصلاحية النهائية للموافقة على الاستثمار في منصة حوكمة؟" |
| "Is there an existing budget line for governance technology, or would this be a new request?" | "هل يوجد بند ميزانية حالي لتقنية الحوكمة، أم سيكون هذا طلباً جديداً?" |
| "Can you introduce us to the decision-maker before we invest in a full demo?" | "هل يمكنكم تقديمنا لصاحب القرار قبل أن نستثمر في عرض توضيحي كامل؟" |

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 15 | Economic buyer identified by name, engaged in the process, and has confirmed budget availability |
| 10 | Economic buyer identified, but no direct engagement yet -- champion is intermediary |
| 5 | "My manager will decide" -- EB identified by role but not by name, no access |
| 0 | No idea who approves purchases. The contact cannot describe the approval process. |

## D -- Decision Criteria (15 points)

**The question:** What will they evaluate us on, and can we influence the criteria?

### Criteria That Favor Musahm (Plant These)

| Criterion | Why It Favors Us | Trap-Setting Question |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|
| Arabic-first interface | Musahm is native Arabic. Competitors localize. | "Is Arabic the primary working language for your Board Secretary and executives?" |
| Shareholder registry integration | Only Musahm has this. | "Do you need your governance platform to also manage shareholder records?" |
| SMS shareholder communications | Musahm-exclusive. | "How do you currently send formal notices to shareholders?" |
| Listed + unlisted + LLC coverage | Competitors focus on listed only. | "Do you manage governance for multiple entity types?" |
| Speed to deploy | Vault: minutes. Ebana: months. Diligent: months. | "How quickly do you need the system operational?" |
| Saudi-built, Saudi-supported | Local team, local hours, local regulations. | "Is it important that your vendor understands Saudi Companies Law natively?" |

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 15 | Decision criteria documented, and 3+ criteria align with Musahm's strengths. We influenced the evaluation framework. |
| 10 | Criteria known but generic ("easy to use, secure, affordable"). Need to sharpen toward our strengths. |
| 5 | "We will evaluate a few options and decide." No formal criteria articulated. |
| 0 | Criteria unknown. Prospect will not share their evaluation framework. |

## D -- Decision Process (10 points)

**The question:** What are the steps from "interested" to "signed contract"?

### Discovery Questions

| Question (EN) | Question (AR) |
|---------------|---------------|
| "Walk me through what happens between today and a signed agreement. Who is involved at each step?" | "أخبرني عن الخطوات من اليوم حتى توقيع الاتفاقية. من يشارك في كل خطوة؟" |
| "Is there a procurement process, or does the CEO approve directly?" | "هل توجد عملية مشتريات، أم يوافق المدير التنفيذي مباشرة؟" |
| "Do you need a trial period before committing, or can you decide after a demo?" | "هل تحتاجون فترة تجريبية قبل الالتزام، أم يمكنكم القرار بعد العرض التوضيحي؟" |
| "Is there a board approval required for technology purchases?" | "هل تحتاج مشتريات التقنية إلى موافقة مجلس الإدارة؟" |

### KSA-Specific Process Notes

- **Family companies:** CEO often decides alone. Fast cycle (1-2 weeks).
- **Closed joint-stock:** Board Secretary recommends, CEO approves, sometimes board ratifies. Medium cycle (2-4 weeks).
- **Listed companies:** Formal procurement, vendor evaluation committee, sometimes RFP. Long cycle (1-3 months).

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Full process mapped: steps, people, timeline, and any legal/procurement requirements. |
| 7 | General process known ("CEO decides after demo") but steps not detailed. |
| 3 | Vague process. "We will get back to you." |
| 0 | No visibility into how they make decisions. |

## I -- Identify Pain (20 points)

**The question:** Is the pain real, urgent, and acknowledged by the economic buyer?

### Pain Categories for KSA Governance Buyers

| Pain Category | Signal Phrases to Listen For | Severity |
|---------------|------------------------------|----------|
| **Regulatory pressure** | "CMA is asking for...", "The new Companies Law requires...", "We had an audit finding..." | Critical |
| **Shareholder disputes** | "A shareholder complained about...", "We had a disagreement over share transfers..." | Critical |
| **Audit trail gaps** | "We could not prove...", "It took weeks to find...", "The auditor asked for records we did not have..." | High |
| **Board Secretary burnout** | "She spends days preparing for each meeting...", "Everything is manual..." | High |
| **Document chaos** | "Files are everywhere...", "We lost a contract...", "No one knows who has the latest version..." | Medium |
| **Growth/investment readiness** | "We are preparing for investment...", "We want to professionalize before the IPO..." | Medium |

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 20 | Pain is specific, quantified, and acknowledged by the economic buyer. There is a triggering event (audit, IPO prep, regulatory change, shareholder dispute). |
| 15 | Pain is real and described in detail by the champion, but the economic buyer has not explicitly confirmed it. |
| 10 | Pain exists but is general ("we should improve our governance"). No triggering event. |
| 5 | Pain is theoretical. "Someday we might need this." |
| 0 | No pain. "We are happy with WhatsApp and Excel." |

## C -- Champion (15 points)

**The question:** Do we have someone inside who is selling for us when we are not in the room?

### Champion Profile for Musahm

The ideal champion is typically the **Board Secretary** (أمين المجلس):
- Lives with governance pain daily
- Has the most to gain from Musahm (their job gets easier)
- Trusted by the CEO on governance matters
- Can articulate the problem to the economic buyer in their language

### Champion Validation Tests

| Test | Question to Ask Yourself | Pass/Fail |
|------|--------------------------|-----------|
| **Access test** | Can they get us a meeting with the economic buyer? | Must pass |
| **Pain test** | Do they describe the governance problem from personal experience, not hypothetically? | Must pass |
| **Advocacy test** | Have they said "I want this" or "we need this" (not just "interesting")? | Must pass |
| **Influence test** | Does the economic buyer listen to them on governance matters? | Should pass |
| **Urgency test** | Are they pushing for a timeline, or is this "whenever"? | Nice to have |

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 15 | Champion validated on all 5 tests. They are actively selling Musahm internally, sharing materials, and pushing for a decision. |
| 10 | Champion passes 3/5 tests. Interested and engaged but not yet actively advocating. |
| 5 | We have a contact who responds to emails but has not demonstrated internal influence. |
| 0 | No internal advocate. We are selling to a wall. |

## Deal Stage Gate Checklist

Use this at each stage transition. A deal cannot advance unless it meets the minimum score.

### Stage 1: Discovery -> Qualification

- [ ] Pain identified and described by the prospect (not assumed by us)
- [ ] At least one persona engaged (Board Secretary, CEO, CFO, or Legal)
- [ ] Company fits ICP: Saudi SMB, 20-500 employees, LLC/closed joint-stock/listed
- [ ] **MEDDIC score: 30+**

### Stage 2: Qualification -> Demo

- [ ] Economic buyer identified by name
- [ ] Decision criteria known (even if general)
- [ ] Champion validated (access test + pain test passed)
- [ ] Competitive landscape mapped
- [ ] **MEDDIC score: 50+**

### Stage 3: Demo -> Proposal

- [ ] Economic buyer has seen or been briefed on the demo
- [ ] Decision process mapped with timeline
- [ ] Metrics quantified (even roughly: "saves 20 hours per board meeting cycle")
- [ ] Champion confirmed they will advocate for Musahm
- [ ] **MEDDIC score: 60+**

### Stage 4: Proposal -> Close

- [ ] Economic buyer has confirmed budget
- [ ] Decision criteria formally aligned to Musahm strengths
- [ ] All competitors addressed with differentiation
- [ ] Legal/procurement requirements identified and met
- [ ] Champion actively pushing for signature
- [ ] **MEDDIC score: 75+**
