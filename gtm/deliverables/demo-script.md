# Musahm Product Demo Script

> **Duration:** 25 minutes (expandable to 40 with Q&A)
> **Audience:** Board Secretary (primary), CEO, CFO, Legal Counsel
> **Structure:** 6 scenes, problem-first, wow-moments built in
> **Pre-requisites:** Demo tenant pre-loaded with sample data (see Appendix A)
> **Last updated:** 2026-04-02

---

## Pre-Demo Checklist

Before every demo, confirm:

- [ ] Demo tenant loaded with sample company data (Appendix A)
- [ ] At least 5 sample shareholders in the registry
- [ ] 2 sample board meetings (one completed with minutes, one upcoming)
- [ ] 3-4 documents in Vault across different workspaces
- [ ] 1 workflow configured (document approval: Submitter -> Reviewer -> Approver)
- [ ] SMS integration active (for live notification send)
- [ ] Prospect's phone number available (for the SMS wow moment)
- [ ] Arabic interface set as default (switch to English only if requested)
- [ ] Screen sharing configured, browser zoomed to 125% for readability

---

## Scene 1: The Hook (3 minutes)

**Purpose:** Establish the prospect's current pain before showing any product.

### Talk Track (EN)

> "Before I show you anything, let me describe what we hear from companies like yours.
>
> Board meetings are coordinated over WhatsApp. Minutes are written in Word. Attendance is tracked in an Excel sheet. Shareholder records live in a spreadsheet that one person maintains. Contracts are emailed as attachments. And when someone asks 'where is the board resolution from March?' -- someone digs through folders for an hour.
>
> Does that sound familiar?"

**[PAUSE. Let the prospect respond. Their answer becomes your anchor for the rest of the demo.]**

### Talk Track (AR)

> "قبل أن أعرض شيئاً، دعوني أصف ما نسمعه من شركات مثل شركتكم.
>
> اجتماعات المجلس تُنسّق عبر واتساب. المحاضر تُكتب في وورد. الحضور يُتابع في جدول إكسل. سجل المساهمين في ملف واحد يحتفظ به شخص واحد. العقود تُرسل كمرفقات بريد. وعندما يسأل أحد 'أين قرار المجلس من مارس؟' -- يبحث شخص في المجلدات لمدة ساعة.
>
> هل يبدو هذا مألوفاً؟"

### Persona Adaptation

| If the audience is... | Emphasize... |
|----------------------|-------------|
| Board Secretary | "You are doing the work of a platform manually." |
| CEO | "Your company's governance record depends on one person's filing system." |
| CFO | "If CMA asks for an audit trail tomorrow, how many days until you can produce it?" |
| Legal Counsel | "WhatsApp messages can be deleted. Excel cells can be overwritten. Neither has legal standing." |

## Scene 2: The Dashboard (2 minutes)

**Purpose:** First impression. Show the full governance picture in one screen.

### Clicks

1. Log in to Musahm (Arabic interface is default)
2. Land on the main dashboard

### Talk Track

> "This is your governance command center. Upcoming board meetings, recent resolutions, shareholder overview, and Vault documents -- all in one view. Your Board Secretary opens this every morning instead of checking 4 different tools."
>
> "Notice the interface is Arabic-first. This is not a translated platform. Every label, every flow, every navigation element was designed in Arabic."

### Wow Moment

> Point to the shareholder count and say: "See that number? That is your live shareholder count, pulled from a system-managed registry. Not an Excel file. A governed record."

---

## Scene 3: Shareholder Registry (5 minutes)

**Purpose:** Show the single biggest differentiator. No competitor has this.

### Clicks

1. Navigate to Shareholder Registry
2. Show the shareholder list with ownership percentages
3. Open one shareholder profile
4. Initiate a mock share transfer
5. Show the transfer history with timestamps

### Talk Track

> "This is the feature no other Saudi governance platform offers.
>
> Every shareholder, their ownership percentage, their contact details, and every share transfer -- logged, timestamped, and auditable. When a shareholder buys or sells, you process the transfer here. The system records who initiated it, when, and the resulting ownership structure.
>
> No Excel sheet. No manual calculation. No 'who updated this last?' questions."

### Talk Track (AR)

> "هذه هي الميزة التي لا تقدمها أي منصة حوكمة سعودية أخرى.
>
> كل مساهم، نسبة ملكيته، بيانات الاتصال، وكل نقل أسهم -- مسجّل وبطابع زمني وقابل للتدقيق. عندما يشتري مساهم أو يبيع، تعالجون النقل هنا. النظام يسجّل من بدأه، ومتى، وهيكل الملكية الناتج.
>
> لا جداول إكسل. لا حسابات يدوية. لا أسئلة 'من حدّث هذا آخر مرة؟'"

### SMS Wow Moment (THE DEMO CLOSER)

> "Now watch this."
>
> 1. Select the shareholder list
> 2. Click "Send SMS Notification"
> 3. Type a sample notification: "Notice: Board meeting scheduled for [date]. Agenda attached."
> 4. If the prospect gave their phone number: send it to them live
>
> **[The prospect's phone buzzes during the meeting. This is the moment they remember.]**
>
> "That SMS is now tracked in the system -- delivery status, timestamp, content, recipient. When CMA asks 'did you notify your shareholders about the capital increase?' -- you show them this log."

## Scene 4: Vault -- Governance Documents (5 minutes)

**Purpose:** Show that Vault is not file storage. It is governance infrastructure.

### Clicks

1. Switch to Vault
2. Show the 6 pre-configured governance workspaces (Board Meetings, Decisions, Committees, Contracts, Policies, General)
3. Open a document in the Board Meetings workspace
4. Show the audit trail on that document
5. Show the version history
6. Demonstrate external sharing with OTP

### Talk Track

> "Vault is not Dropbox. Vault is not SharePoint. Vault organizes documents the way governance works.
>
> Six workspaces, pre-configured: Board Meetings, Decisions, Committees, Contracts, Policies, and General. Every document sits in the context of the governance function it serves.
>
> This board resolution -- I can see who uploaded it, who opened it, who edited it, and when. Every action has a permanent record. No one can delete, edit, or access a document without the system knowing."

### OTP Sharing Wow Moment

> 1. Click "Share Externally" on a document
> 2. Show the OTP verification form and watermark preview
>
> "When you share a document with an external party -- an auditor, a legal advisor, a shareholder -- they receive an OTP code to their email. The document they see has a watermark with their name and the date. If it leaks, you know who shared it and when."

---

## Scene 5: Workflows & AI (3 minutes)

**Purpose:** Show automation that replaces manual approval chains.

### Clicks

1. Open a workflow template (Submitter -> Reviewer -> Approver)
2. Show a document going through the workflow
3. Briefly show AI extraction on an uploaded document

### Talk Track

> "When a contract needs approval, you do not email PDFs back and forth. You submit it in Vault, the workflow assigns it to the reviewer, then the approver. Every step is logged. If someone skips a step, the system blocks it.
>
> And when your team uploads a contract or invoice, the AI extracts key fields automatically -- date, amount, parties, reference number. Your team reviews and approves the extraction. No manual data entry."

### Honest Roadmap Moment

> "We are also building two features that we are excited about: semantic search -- which will find documents by meaning, not just keywords -- and Ask Vault, which will let you ask questions about your document library in natural language. These are under active development and will be available in a future release. Today, keyword search works across all fields and finds any document in seconds."

## Scene 6: The Close (4 minutes)

**Purpose:** Tie everything back to the prospect's pain, then ask for the next step.

### Talk Track Template

> "Let me connect what you have seen to what you told me at the start.
>
> You said [repeat their exact pain from Scene 1].
>
> What you just saw is: [connect each pain point to a feature]
> - Your WhatsApp board coordination -> one platform
> - Your Excel shareholder registry -> system-managed, auditable registry
> - Your scattered documents -> governed workspaces with full audit trail
> - Your manual notifications -> tracked SMS from the platform
>
> We have 30 beta seats remaining. I would like to get your company set up this week. The setup takes 15 minutes. Who else should be involved in that conversation?"
